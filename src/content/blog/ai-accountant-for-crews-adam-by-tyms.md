## What Adam by Tyms actually does (in normal words)

Adam is built to do three main jobs for a small business:

1. **Ingest your money flow**  
   - Connect bank accounts, or upload bank statements  
   - Pull in transactions automatically  

2. **Clean and organize the data**  
   - Categorize income and expenses  
   - Keep a structured chart of accounts  
   - Maintain the ledgers and journals in the background  

3. **Spit out the numbers you or your accountant care about**  
   - Income statement, balance sheet, cash flow, trial balance  
   - Dashboards that show how the business is doing  
   - Answers to questions like:  
     - “How much did we spend on fuel this month”  
     - “Which client or job has the highest margin”  

You talk to it like a person (web, WhatsApp, email), and it does the bookkeeping heavy lifting.

You can use Adam as:

- A standalone accounting system, or  
- An AI layer on top of tools like QuickBooks or Xero, where it syncs back and forth instead of forcing a full migration  

## Where it fits for crew-based businesses

For a typical 5–50 person crew business (construction, field services, cleaning, etc.), the stack usually looks like:

- **Time tracking and scheduling** → something like TimeCamp or Connecteam  
- **Payroll and HR** → something like WebHR or a payroll provider  
- **Accounting** → QuickBooks, Xero, or whatever you have hacked together  

Adam is trying to live in that third slot.

Good fits:

- You want **clean books and basic financial reports** without becoming your own bookkeeper  
- You often **fall behind on categorizing transactions** because you would rather be in the field or selling  
- You want to **ask questions about your numbers in normal language** instead of wrestling with reports  

Not a great fit:

- You already have an accountant and bookkeeper who are on top of things and you like the current workflow  
- You do heavy, custom job costing inside another system and do not want one more tool in the mix  
- You are allergic to anything AI flavored and just want classic accounting software

## Cons and things to watch

No tool is magic, and Adam has tradeoffs you should know about:

- **You still need to care a little.**  
  If nobody ever checks what the AI is doing, errors can sit in your books. It lightens the load, it does not remove ownership.  

- **Bank sync can still be imperfect at times.**  
  Like any modern fintech tool, you may occasionally need to upload statements manually.  

- **AI credits and usage limits.**  
  Some plans use a credit model for chats, transactions, and document uploads, so heavy users may need higher tiers.  

- **It is not built just for construction.**  
  You may need to work with your accountant to make sure job costing and your chart of accounts match how you think about jobs and crews.

## Common questions from owners

**Can AI accountants handle payroll for small crews?**  
AI accounting tools like Adam focus mainly on bookkeeping and financial reporting. They do not process payroll directly. You still need a payroll provider or software to handle wages, taxes, and compliance. However, Adam can integrate with payroll data to help you see payroll costs in your financial reports.

**Is AI accounting software secure for small business financial data?**  
Security is a top priority for reputable AI accounting tools. Adam uses encryption and secure connections to protect your data. Still, it’s wise to review their security policies and ensure you use strong passwords and two-factor authentication where available.

**Can small crew owners automate tax filing with AI accountants?**  
Most AI accounting tools do not fully automate tax filing. They can prepare reports and organize data to make tax time easier, but you will still need to file taxes yourself or work with an accountant. Some platforms may offer add-ons or integrations for tax filing, but check what’s included before relying on it.

**Will using Adam replace my accountant or bookkeeper?**  
Adam can reduce the time you spend on bookkeeping and give you clearer financial insights, but it does not replace professional accountants or bookkeepers. You still need someone to review your books, handle complex tax issues, and provide advice. Think of Adam as a tool to make their job easier and your financial picture clearer.

**How much training or setup does Adam require?**  
Adam is designed to be user-friendly, with natural language interaction and automated categorization. You will need to connect bank accounts and review categories regularly at first. Over time, it learns your preferences and requires less hands-on work.
